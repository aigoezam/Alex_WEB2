About Me

I'm Alexander Immanuel Goerke, graduate in Industrial Electronics and Automation Engineering from Universitat Politècnica de València (UPV), having just completed an Erasmus+ exchange at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU).

I enjoy the full stack of an engineering project: doing the math, writing the code, wiring the hardware, and debugging it on the bench until it works. Bachelor Thesis

Differential Robot Arm — Control Design

My thesis project: writing and testing the control code for a differential robotic arm, for agricultural weed-intervention research, from a blank sketch to a robot moving on a bench.

🤖 Real-robot control, not just simulation 📐 Full IK/FK derivation 🎓 Prototype now used in DTU research
  1. Define robot geometry & coordinate frames
  2. Derive & validate inverse/forward kinematics
  3. Simulate the full control pipeline in Python
  4. Port the controller to C++ on a Teensy 4.1
  5. Log real-robot experiments and compare controllers

Default mode: encoder-based bounded feedback correcting the inverse-kinematics reference — the main demonstration mode used in the thesis.
