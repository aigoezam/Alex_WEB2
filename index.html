Who I am

About Me

I'm Alexander Immanuel Goerke, an Industrial Electronics and Automation Engineer from Universitat Politècnica de València, with a year specializing in robotics and digital control at the Technical University of Denmark. I'm driven by investigating technical problems end to end, from theoretical development and simulation through to implementation in industrial environments and continuous process improvement.

My Bachelor's thesis focused on the geometric and software design of a differential robotic arm for an agricultural project, covering everything from the kinematics to a C++ controller running on a Teensy 4.1. The prototype is still in use today within a PhD research project at the Technical University of Denmark.

During my final year at university, following a selection process, I joined Cebreo Medical as a Software Engineer, a medical technology company in Copenhagen, where I developed tools to visualize biomedical signals (EEG, PPG, accelerometry) alongside a multidisciplinary team, enabling people without a technical background to study human body signals for medical device development.

I work with Python, C/C++, MATLAB/Simulink and design tools such as SolidWorks. I'm currently looking for my next challenge in robotics, control and process automation within the tech industry.

Bachelor Thesis

Delta Robot: Control Design

My thesis project: writing and testing the control code for a delta robot, for agricultural weed-intervention research, from a blank sketch to a robot moving on a bench.

🤖 Real-robot control, not just simulation 📐 Full IK/FK derivation 🎯 1-2 mm end-effector accuracy 🎓 Prototype now used in research at the Technical University of Denmark
  1. Define robot geometry & coordinate frames
  2. Derive & validate inverse/forward kinematics
  3. Simulate the full control pipeline in Python
  4. Port the controller to C++ on a Teensy 4.1
  5. Log real-robot experiments and compare controllers

Default mode: encoder-based bounded feedback correcting the inverse-kinematics reference. This is the main demonstration mode used in the thesis.
